the HIGH COST
of HIGH FLYING

The Internet has revolutionized nearly every industry on the planet, and basketball shoes are no exception. Recently, young sneakerheads have found themselves paying hundreds and thousands of dollars for kicks - that is, when they can even find them for sale. Long lines and quick online sellouts have led to a burgeoning resale market. eBay, Craigslist, and social media sites have been the way for many to find their fashionable footwear. But where the market is free, it can be costly.

RETAIL PRICES AREN'T GOING UP

In fact, when adjusted for inflation, the price of the most popular shoes wasn’t higher over the last fifteen years than it was in the ’80s and ’90s. The Air Jordan line has released a shoe every year but two since 1985, and prices have remained pretty consistent.

SO WHY ARE THEY SO EXPENSIVE?

Shoes are released in limited supply, sometimes as surprise releases.

Hackers have developed bots that buy up new releases automatically to resell.

Resellers mark up prices by hundreds or thousands of dollars.

THE DEVIL’S IN THE RESALE

Anticipated sneakers are released in such limited supply, many people can't get them directly. Online resales make up an increasingly significant section of the sneaker industry, accounting for nearly a quarter of a billion dollars in 2014 - a quarter of that on eBay.

LAISSEZ FAIRE JORDAN

Even as retail prices of Jordan line shoes remain stagnant, the average buyer is paying about 55% more to buy them, thanks to low supply in a free online market.

YEEZY JUST JUMPED OVER JUMPMAN

Rapper Kanye West released some of the world's most high-profile sneakers. His "Air Yeezy II Red Octobers" sold in 2014 for just $250 retail but an average of $5,979 online in the past 12 months.
